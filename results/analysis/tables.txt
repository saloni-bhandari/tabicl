7. ASSESSMENT — IMPACT, NOVELTY, AND FRAMING
----------------------------------------

  IMPACT:
  • The primary value is runtime reduction on large datasets (n > 30K).
    5/8 large datasets achieve >1.5x speedup with ≤2% acc drop.
  • On small/medium datasets, localization adds overhead and is not beneficial
    for runtime — though it can sometimes improve accuracy by focusing the
    ICL context on the most relevant training samples.
  • Memory savings are inconsistent. The caching overhead can offset gains
    from smaller Stage 3 inputs.

  NOVELTY:
  • The core idea — row-selective ICL via KNN in embedding space — is a
    natural extension of TabICL's architecture. It is not deeply novel but
    is a sensible, well-motivated engineering contribution.
  • The comparison between embedding-space and raw-feature-space retrieval
    provides useful empirical insight into whether learned representations
    capture task-relevant structure for nearest-neighbor selection.
  • The approach relates to retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) in NLP,
    applying similar intuitions to tabular in-context learning.

  RECOMMENDED FRAMING:
  • Frame as: 'Retrieval-Augmented In-Context Learning for Tabular Data'
    — positioning it at the intersection of ICL and retrieval-augmented methods.
  • Lead with the SCALABILITY story: full TabICL becomes impractical at
    large n_train due to quadratic attention cost. Localized TabICL makes
    ICL-based tabular prediction feasible at scale.
  • Emphasize the accuracy-retention result: on most datasets, localization
    retains baseline accuracy while substantially reducing inference time.
  • The emb-vs-raw comparison is a secondary but interesting finding. If
    embedding retrieval consistently wins, this validates that Stages 1-2
    learn useful representations beyond what raw features provide.
  • Be honest about limitations: overhead on small datasets, memory tradeoffs,
    and the fact that this is an engineering optimization rather than a
    fundamental algorithmic advance.

8. KEY RECOMMENDATIONS FOR REPORT
----------------------------------------
  • Include a clear 'when to use' guideline: Localized TabICL is recommended
    when n_train > 10K–15K and fast inference is needed.
  • Show the Pareto plots (accuracy vs speedup) prominently — they
    communicate the tradeoff more clearly than tables.
  • Report k=32 or k=64 as the recommended default — these typically offer
    the best accuracy-speed tradeoff.
  • The timing breakdown (Stage 1-2 / Retrieval / Stage 3) shows where time
    is spent and why localization helps — Stage 3 cost drops dramatically.
